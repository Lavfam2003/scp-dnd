What Do I Do On My Turn?

Combat Medic

START → Is someone at 0 HP?
  YES → Can you reach them?
    YES → First Aid (action) + Triage (BA)
    NO → Trauma Extraction (action: grab + drag + patch)
      or Combat Stabilization (reaction: throw canister)
  NO → Is someone hurt?
    YES → First Aid (action) + Triage (BA) on worst off
    NO → Attack (action) or hold

ALWAYS CHECK: Got Triage charges? Use BA to patch. Out of charges? Soft landing (1 last stabilize/condition use).
REACTION: Ally drops? Combat Stab OR Emergency Response (pick one).

Internal Medic

START → Pick your mode (one per action):
  Someone bleeding badly? → HEAL (roll First Aid dice)
  Someone has a condition? → CURE (Purge it, costs charge)
  Ally Poisoned/Stunned? → BUFF (Pain Suppression / Steady / Focus)

BONUS ACTION → Pick one:
  Scan enemy (Clinical Scanner → +1 attack for allies on success)
  Coach ally (Directed Triage → ally spends hit die + your WIS)

REACTION: Enemy attacking ally? Analysis Paralysis (disadvantage).
Ally drops to 0 HP? Crash Cart (burn compound → auto-stabilize at 1 HP).

Memetic Specialist

START → Are there mental threats?
  YES → Ally compromised? Cog Decontam (action: re-save w/ advantage)
    Need area defense? Barrier (action: 15ft adv WIS saves)
    Need to shut one down? Cognitive Strike (action: overload/compel/dissociate)
  NO → Someone hurt? First Aid (action)
    Nobody hurt? First Aid on self or attack

BONUS ACTION → Pick one:
  Scan enemy (Psych Reading → CHOOSE: Targeting Data (+dmg) or Tunnel Vision (hide ally))
  Scan ally (Cog Triage → advantage on next WIS/INT save)

REACTION: Ally makes WIS save? Cog Warning (advantage, from L7).

1 Level 1 First Aid, Stabilize, Medical Assessment ▶
First Aid
Action Adjacent
Apply Foundation-grade trauma care: hemostatic gel, dermal adhesive strips, pressure bandages, analgesic autoinjector. Heals 2d6 + WIS modifier HP. Scaling differs by specialization starting at level 5.
Full Details

Apply Foundation-grade trauma care to a creature you can touch: hemostatic gel to seal wounds, dermal adhesive strips, pressure bandages, and a standard-dose analgesic autoinjector. Heals 2d6 + WIS modifier HP.

Scaling differs by specialization starting at level 5 (see individual class tables). Higher-level healing reflects access to better compounds — advanced biofoam sealants, regenerative dermal patches, and Foundation pharmacological stimulants that accelerate natural clotting and tissue repair.

Stabilize
Bonus Action Adjacent
Deploy emergency trauma patch on a dying creature. Target stops making death saves and stabilizes at 0 HP. They're not conscious or healed — the patch is buying time.
Full Details

Deploy an emergency trauma patch on a dying creature within 5 feet — a pre-loaded adhesive pad containing coagulant gel, a mild stimulant, and a pressure-reactive compression layer. The target stops making death saves and stabilizes at 0 HP.

They're not conscious or healed — the patch is buying time until proper treatment.

Medical Assessment
Exploration Examine a Creature
Biometric scanner + hands-on exam. Learn current HP, active conditions, toxins, infections, or anomalous contamination markers. DM may require Medicine check for anomalous effects.
Full Details

Use your biometric scanner and a hands-on examination to evaluate a creature. The scanner reads pulse rate, blood pressure, body temperature, blood oxygen, and pupil response.

Cross-referenced with Foundation medical databases (if available) or your own training, this gives you their current HP, active conditions, toxins, infections, or anomalous contamination markers.

The DM may require a Medicine check for anomalous effects (DC set by the anomaly) — your scanner flags abnormal readings but can't always interpret them.

2 Level 2 Emergency Response, Medic's Eye ▶
Emergency Response
Reaction 30ft Range
When an ally within 30 feet takes damage, move up to 15 feet toward them without provoking opportunity attacks. Must end closer to the ally. Drilled into every Foundation medic from day one.
Full Details

When an ally within 30 feet takes damage, you can use your reaction to move up to 15 feet toward them without provoking opportunity attacks. This movement must end closer to the injured ally than where you started.

You can't use this to move away from danger — only toward the patient.

This is drilled into every Foundation medic from day one: when someone gets hit, you move.

Medic's Eye
Bonus Action 30ft Range Medicine Check (enemies)
On an ally: Exact current HP, max HP, and all active conditions. No check required.
On an enemy: Condition tier (healthy/wounded/bloodied/critical) is free. Scanner detail requires a Medicine check — DC set by the DM based on the creature. On failure, the scanner returns noise — you know the tier but can't interpret the detail.
Full Details

Use your biometric scanner to perform a quick tactical assessment of one creature within 30 feet.

On an ally: You learn their exact current HP, max HP, and any common conditions affecting them that the DM would normally share publicly (poisoned, dazed, frightened, etc.). If something else is affecting them beyond common conditions, your scanner may indicate abnormal readings but you lack the training to interpret what they mean. The DM determines what, if anything, the scanner reveals beyond common conditions.

On an enemy: You learn their condition tier — healthy (75-100%), wounded (50-74%), bloodied (25-49%), or critical (below 25%) — this is free, no check required. For the scanner detail (one DM-narrated piece of intel — stress hormones, thermal signature, neural activity, etc.), make a Medicine check with a DC set by the DM based on the creature's difficulty and your distance from them. Up close (within 10 feet) is easiest — you can read posture, breathing, micro-expressions. At range the DC increases as details become harder to pick out. On failure, the scanner returns noise — you know the tier but can't interpret the detail.

Scanner results are provided to you by the DM privately. You decide what information to share with the party and how to relay it in-character.

The DM determines what the scanner detects. Some entities, anomalies, or situations may cause the scanner to return incomplete data, contradictory readings, or nothing at all — this is your equipment reaching its limits, not a ruling to argue against.

This is the base-level version of what specializations will later enhance.

Condition tier is always free so scanning is never wasted. The check gates the useful intel behind enemy difficulty — harder creatures are harder to read.

3 Level 3 — Specialization Entry Triage, Combat Stabilization ▶
Triage
Bonus Action WIS mod / SR Touch
Slap a quick-deploy trauma patch. Heals 1d6 + WIS modifier HP. WIS modifier charges per short rest.

Soft landing: When your last charge is spent, retain one additional use for stabilize (auto, no healing) or condition removal only. Then fully expended.
Full Details

Slap a quick-deploy trauma patch on a creature you can touch — a pre-loaded adhesive pad containing hemostatic gel and a mild analgesic. Heals 1d6 + WIS modifier HP.

Uses: WIS modifier per short rest (minimum 1). At WIS 14 (+2), that's 2 patches. At WIS 16 (+3), that's 3.

Side effects: Localized numbness at the application site for ~10 minutes, mild inflammation. Repeated application to the same area within an hour can cause chemical burns from the hemostatic gel.

Soft landing: When your last Triage charge is spent, you retain one additional use of Triage that can only be used to stabilize a creature at 0 HP (auto-stabilize, no healing) or remove one condition (as Patch Job, if available). This final use heals no HP — you're out of the good supplies but you still have your training. After this use, Triage is fully expended until your next short rest.

Side effects: localized numbness ~10 min, mild inflammation. Repeated application → chemical burns.
Combat Stabilization
Reaction 1 / SR 30ft Range
When ally drops to 0 HP, throw a stabilization canister. DEX check — DC 8 (15ft), DC 12 (30ft), DC 16 (40ft at L7+). Success = auto-stabilize. Choose one reaction per trigger — can't use with Emergency Response on same event.
Full Details

When an ally within 30 feet (40 feet at level 7+) drops to 0 HP, you can use your reaction to throw an impact-triggered hemostatic foam canister. Make a DEX check — the DC scales with distance:

Within 15 feet: DC 8 (easy toss)
Within 30 feet: DC 12 (solid throw under pressure)
Within 40 feet (level 7+): DC 16 (long-range hail mary)

On success, the target automatically stabilizes. On failure, the canister lands nearby but doesn't deploy properly — the target must still make death saves.

Reaction choice: This is a new reaction option alongside Emergency Response (which you still have). When an ally drops, you choose one: move toward them (Emergency Response) or stabilize at range (Combat Stabilization). You cannot use both on the same trigger — one reaction per event.

4 Level 4 Under Fire, Proximity Training, Field Forensics, TMA ▶
Under Fire
Passive
Moving to heal/stabilize an ally does not provoke opportunity attacks. When you use First Aid or Triage, move up to half speed as part of that action without provoking.
Full Details

Moving to heal or stabilize an ally does not provoke opportunity attacks, but you must declare your intent to heal a specific target before moving. If you move under this passive and do not heal the declared target, the DM may rule that the movement provoked opportunity attacks.

If you use First Aid or Triage on a creature, you can move up to half your speed as part of that action/bonus action without provoking.

Pure training, no tech. You're just very good at not dying in firefights.

Proximity Training
Passive10ft
Allies within 10ft who drop to 0 HP choose: Safe (+1 to death saves) or Gamble (2 fails/2 successes, regain 1d4+CON on self-stabilize). Persists even if you move away.
Full Details

When an ally drops to 0 HP within 10 feet of you, your combat medical training steadies them. They choose one of the following effects, which persists for the entire time they are downed even if you move away afterward:

Safe: +1 bonus to death saving throws. They know help is close.

Gamble: Death save thresholds decrease to 2 fails / 2 successes (normally 3/3). If they stabilize through their own saves, they regain 1d4 + CON modifier HP instead of staying at 0 — they're back on their feet without anyone spending an action. High risk: two failed saves and they're gone. High reward: two successes and they're up.

The choice is made when the ally drops. One word to the DM: "safe" or "gamble."

Field Forensics
Exploration — Examine Body/Scene
Examine bodies/scenes. Medicine check — the DM determines what you learn based on your roll. Higher rolls reveal more: cause of death, time of death, toxicology, whether the scene was staged.
Full Details

Your medical training extends to analyzing injuries, wounds, and cause of death. You can perform a field-expedient forensic examination of a body, crime scene, or injury to determine:

• Cause and mechanism of death/injury (weapon type, caliber, anomalous energy signature based on wound characteristics)
• Approximate time of death (based on lividity, temperature, rigor — field estimates, not lab precision)
• Self-inflicted wounds (wound angle, hesitation marks, powder burns)
• Obvious inconsistencies between injuries and the reported narrative
• Toxicology assessment (identifying common poisons, pharmaceutical agents, or anomalous compounds in tissue)

Make a Medicine check. The DM determines what you learn based on your roll — higher rolls reveal more detail and more subtle observations.

This is a preliminary field assessment, not a formal forensic investigation — Security Division will conduct the official review later if needed.

Tactical Medical Assessment
Before Going In
Flat d20 (no modifiers) — prep the team's self-aid gear and brief injury expectations. DC 10+: party gains +1 to death saving throws for the mission. Nat 20: DM provides a medical-reasoning tactical insight. Roll 1-5: misinformation or false confidence.
Full Details

You prep the team for what you think might happen — staging tourniquets, marking blood types, briefing self-aid procedures. This is experience and instinct, not guaranteed expertise. You don't know what you're walking into.

Roll a flat d20 (no proficiency or ability modifiers):

1-2: Your assessment is wrong. DM determines the specific penalty.
3-5: Inconclusive. No benefit, no penalty.
6-9: Basic prep. No mechanical benefit, but you staged gear competently.
10+: Solid assessment. All party members gain +1 to death saving throws for this mission.
Nat 20: Exceptional read. +1 death saves plus the DM provides one piece of medical-tactical insight.

Does not stack with IM's Classified Consultation or MS's CRV baselines.

5 Level 5 Adrenaline Shot, Trauma Extraction, FA 3d6 ▶
Adrenaline Shot (Compound 47)
Bonus Action 1 / LR Adjacent, Willing
2d6 temp HP, advantage on next attack, +10ft speed. Duration: target's second turn after injection.
Mental override: If target is Stunned/Dominated/Charmed/Feared, forces immediate re-save (no advantage).
Failure rider: Even on failed re-save, advantage on next natural save against that effect.
Crash: Disadvantage on attacks + ability checks until end of next turn.
Full Details

Compound 47 combat stimulant via autoinjector — military-grade synthetic catecholamine cocktail, adrenaline dialed up to 11.

Effect: Administer to a willing target within 5 feet. The target gains:

• 2d6 temporary HP (pain suppression and vasoconstriction)
• Advantage on their next attack roll (heightened reflexes)
• +10 feet movement speed (stimulant-driven muscle response)
• Duration: until the end of the target's second turn after injection

Crash: When the effect ends, the target has disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks until the end of their next turn. Shaking hands, tunnel vision, labored breathing. Cannot be prevented or reduced.

Consent requirement: The target must verbally consent before injection. They must be conscious, able to speak, and not under any compulsion. A Silenced target cannot consent. A Dominated creature cannot give valid consent.

Mental Override: If the target is currently affected by Stun, Domination, Charm, or Fear, the adrenaline shock forces an immediate re-save against the effect (no advantage). The Foundation built a pharmacological counter — a mild anti-psychotic compound — directly into the stimulant cocktail.

Failure rider: If the mental override re-save fails, the target gains advantage on their next natural save against that same effect.

Side effects: elevated heart rate for 1 hour, mild hand tremor, appetite suppression. A second dose within 24 hours risks cardiac arrest (DM discretion).

Authorization: Compound 47 is a restricted Foundation combat stimulant. Each dose is tracked by serial number.

Target must be conscious, able to speak, and not under compulsion. Silenced/Dominated creatures cannot consent.
Trauma Extraction
Action NEW v0.12 1 / SR Adjacent
Grab willing/unconscious creature adjacent to you. Move your full speed dragging them — no opportunity attacks for either of you.
At 0 HP: Medicine check to stabilize (DC set by DM). Failure = they remain unstable.
End of movement (stable + conscious only): Free Triage at reduced rate (one die step lower). Costs a charge.
Behind cover: Advantage on death saves until your next turn.
L9 upgrade: Can use as bonus action (no free Triage rider).
Full Details

You cross open ground with a body on your shoulder because nobody else will.

Choose a willing or unconscious creature within 5 feet. You drag or carry that creature with you as you move up to your full movement speed. Neither you nor the creature provokes opportunity attacks during this movement.

Stabilization while moving: If the creature is at 0 HP, you attempt to stabilize them while moving — make a Medicine check (DC set by DM). On success, the creature stabilizes at 0 HP. On failure, they remain unstable and continue making death saves. You are applying a tourniquet while sprinting — it doesn't always work.

Free Triage on arrival: At the end of this movement, if the extracted creature is stable and conscious, you may use Triage as a free action (costs a Triage charge). This Triage heals at a reduced rate (one die step lower) — you're patching on the move, not working in ideal conditions.

If you end the movement behind half cover or better, the extracted creature gains advantage on death saving throws until the start of your next turn.

Limitations: Uses your action — you cannot First Aid or attack on the same turn. Once per short rest.

Side effects: significant physical strain — after using Trauma Extraction, you have disadvantage on Athletics and Acrobatics checks until the end of your next turn.

Interaction — Under Fire (L4): Under Fire covers your approach; Trauma Extraction covers the retreat with a body. Emergency Response (L2) gets you in (reaction move toward downed ally); Trauma Extraction gets you both out (next turn).

Emergency Response gets you in. Trauma Extraction gets you both out. 1/SR + med check + healing penalty keeps this as a dramatic rescue, not a rotation ability.

Patch Job + Field Remedy

Full Details

When you use Triage, you can apply one of these specialized variants instead of (or in addition to) the standard healing patch. Each variant costs a Triage charge.

Variants:

• Ocular flush capsule: Removes Blinded. Side effect: eyes water for 5 minutes.
• Aural stabilizer: Removes Deafened. Side effect: faint ringing for 10 minutes.
• Toxin binder: Removes Poisoned. Side effect: localized discoloration for 24 hours.
• Field Remedy (smelling salts / neural reset): Removes Frightened OR allows target to immediately repeat a saving throw against Charmed (no advantage on the re-save — just a free repeat). Side effect: brief disorientation (disadvantage on next ability check).

Field Remedy is ammonia inhalant combined with a mild adrenal stimulant — it shocks the nervous system out of a panic state or gives a charmed mind a jolt of clarity. Crude compared to what the Internal Medic or Memetic Specialist can do, but it's something.

Field Remedy only handles Frightened and Charmed — the two most common mental conditions in combat. It does NOT work on Stunned, Incapacitated, or anomalous mental effects. For those, you need an Internal Medic or Memetic Specialist.

This is Tunnel Vision scaled up: instead of one signal thread, the scanner runs N enemies × up to 4 allies worth of parallel calculations.

RULES & REFERENCE

Key rules, timing reference, institutional oversight

Key Rules

Anomalous vs Non-Anomalous: If the source has an SCP designation or is clearly supernatural, its effects are anomalous. Mundane humans, animals, conventional traps/tech = non-anomalous. DM has final say on edge cases.

Concentration: One effect at a time. CON save on damage (DC 10 or half damage taken, whichever is higher). Can end voluntarily.

Adjacent: All abilities marked "Adjacent" mean you must be next to the target. No distance tracking needed.

Scene: One continuous situation — from when you arrive or something triggers until the threat resolves, you leave, or the DM moves to the next situation. The DM determines when a scene ends.

Shared Equipment Rule: Allies can use basic trauma patches from a downed medic (no proficiency bonus). Specialization equipment requires training — 50% chance of incorrect use without it.
