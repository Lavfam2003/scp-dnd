SCP-012 Euclid "A Bad Composition"
Incomplete sheet music that compels anyone who views it to finish the composition -- in their own blood.
Vector Visual Compulsion
Containment Cover document or destroy it. Break line of sight immediately.
Encounter RP / Compulsion
MS Toolkit
SCRAMBLE Visual Filter
Without MS Close your eyes, cover the document. Physically restrain affected personnel.
Threat Summary
Affected subjects become single-minded in their compulsion to complete the composition. They will use any sharp object to open their own veins and write with the blood. The music is never complete -- the compulsion never ends on its own. Without intervention, death from blood loss occurs within minutes.

Discovery Scenario

The document is found in a containment wing archive or carried by a compromised researcher. Its appearance is unremarkable -- yellowed staff paper with red-brown notation that appears hand-written.

Mechanics

  • Viewing the document: WIS Save DC 14 or be compelled to approach and begin writing
  • Compelled characters use their action each turn to write, dealing 1d4 slashing to themselves
  • Repeat save at end of each turn with cumulative -1 penalty per turn spent writing
  • Covering the document or removing it from line of sight ends the compulsion immediately
  • Destroying the document: AC 11, 5 HP (vulnerable to fire)

DM Notes

The real danger is surprise. If only one character enters the room and fails, no one else may know what's happening. Use sound cues -- the scratching of a pen, the drip of blood. The composition is hauntingly beautiful even unfinished. Characters who succeed their save still feel the pull.

SCP-055 Keter "Unknown"
An antimemetic anomaly. You can't remember what it is. You can't remember that you can't remember.
Vector Memory / Antimemetic
Containment Already contained -- you just forget that it is. Mnestics restore recall.
Encounter Puzzle
MS Toolkit
Mnestics Memory Anchor
Without MS Negative-definition trick: catalog what it ISN'T to triangulate what it is.
Threat Summary
The party will walk into the containment cell, interact with the object, and then walk out having forgotten everything. Repeat. Without mnestics or the negative-definition approach, the party can lose hours or entire sessions to a memory loop they don't know they're in.

Discovery Scenario

The party finds a containment cell that is occupied according to records, but no one can recall what's inside. Notes in the file are blank or contradictory. The door is unlocked -- who would lock something they can't remember exists?

Mechanics

  • Any memory of SCP-055 fades 1 minute after breaking line of sight
  • No saving throw -- the effect is absolute (this is the anomaly's nature, not a spell)
  • Mnestic drugs: grant 1 hour of antimemetic resistance
  • Negative-definition: INT Check DC 16 to realize you can define it by exclusion ("it's not round, not small, not alive...")
  • Written notes about 055 fade from paper within minutes. Photos come out blank.

DM Notes

This encounter requires DM discipline. When a character leaves the room, roleplay their forgetting. Ask "What were you doing?" and if they say "checking the cell," ask "What cell?" The negative-definition trick is a real SCP community solution -- reward players who figure it out. The antimemetic nature also makes this a fantastic plot device: 055 could be hiding critical intel.

SCP-2521 Keter "●●|●●●●●|●●|●"
An information hazard entity. Speaking or writing about it causes it to manifest and take the speaker or the written record.
Vector Information Hazard
Containment Communicate only in pictograms and symbols. Never write or speak about it.
Encounter Communication Puzzle
MS Toolkit
Infohazard Protocol Symbol Training
Without MS Figure out the pictogram rule through trial and error. Stop talking.
Threat Summary
The entity manifests within seconds of verbal or written description. Written records are taken. Speakers are taken. The party must communicate about the threat without triggering it -- a real-time puzzle where the players themselves must follow the rule. One slip and someone disappears.

Discovery Scenario

The party finds a containment document written entirely in pictograms. An NPC begins to explain what the document is about -- and is taken mid-sentence by long black tendrils that emerge from nowhere. The document remains. The NPC is gone.

Mechanics

  • Speaking about the entity: it manifests in 1 round and takes the speaker (no save -- containment failure)
  • Writing about it: the written object is taken in 1 round
  • Pictograms, gestures, and symbolic communication are safe
  • The entity is not hostile -- it "loves" information about itself. It collects, not attacks.
  • Taken creatures are not necessarily dead but are unrecoverable without major plot intervention

DM Notes

This is a player-level puzzle, not a character-level one. The players themselves must figure out how to discuss strategy without triggering the entity. Encourage gestures, drawings, and charades at the table. If a player slips up and describes it verbally, give them a half-second warning ("you feel the air change") before following through. This encounter is memorable precisely because the meta-restriction is real.

SCP-1471 Euclid "MalO ver1.0.0"
A perceptual anomaly entity that appears in peripheral vision with increasing frequency, delivered through a contaminated device.
Vector Perception / Visual
Containment Destroy the contaminated device. Early detection prevents progression.
Encounter Slow Burn
MS Toolkit
CRV Progression Scanner
Without MS Notice behavioral changes in the affected player over time. Destroy the device.
Threat Summary
The entity's appearances escalate across sessions: reflections, then doorways, then constant peripheral presence. The affected character becomes increasingly paranoid and isolated. At terminal stage, the entity is visible at all times and the character can no longer distinguish it from reality. There is no combat -- only creeping psychological deterioration.

Discovery Scenario

A character finds or is given a device (PDA, tablet, anomalous artifact) running unfamiliar software. Using it triggers the contamination. The DM should drip-feed sightings privately to the affected player over multiple sessions.

Mechanics

  • Stage 1 (Session 1-2): Sightings in mirrors and reflective surfaces. WIS perception bonus -1
  • Stage 2 (Session 3-4): Appears in doorways, crowds. WIS perception bonus -2, disadvantage on WIS saves vs fear
  • Stage 3 (Session 5+): Constant presence. WIS score reduced by 2, cannot be surprised (entity warns of threats)
  • Destroying the device at any stage halts progression but does not reverse existing effects
  • CRV baseline scan detects the anomaly at Stage 1, before behavioral symptoms manifest

DM Notes

The power of this encounter is in the slow build. Pass private notes to the affected player. Let the other players slowly notice something is wrong through RP, not mechanics. The entity itself is not hostile -- it is arguably trying to bond. The horror is the loss of agency and the inability to be alone. Stage 3 is deliberately double-edged: you're never alone, but you also can't be ambushed.

SCP-XXXX-A Euclid "The Echo"
A self-propagating auditory cognitohazard. Starts as ringing, becomes words, becomes commands, becomes broadcasting.
Vector Auditory Cognitohazard
Containment Silence all carriers. Locate and destroy the source.
Encounter Spreading Infection
MS Toolkit
Barrier Scanner Pattern Disrupt
Without MS Earplugs for prevention, find the source by triangulation. Gag carriers.
Threat Summary
The Echo spreads exponentially. Each carrier begins broadcasting within minutes, creating new carriers. In enclosed spaces like a Foundation site, a single uncontained carrier can compromise an entire wing. The sound is compelling -- affected individuals don't realize they've started humming, then speaking, then shouting the pattern.

Discovery Scenario

The party enters an area and hears a faint ringing. An NPC nearby is humming softly, seemingly unaware. Another NPC further away is speaking rapidly in a monotone. The source is deeper in the facility.

Mechanics

  • Exposure: hearing the sound for more than 1 round requires WIS Save DC 13
  • Stage 1 -- Ringing: Tinnitus, -2 to perception (hearing)
  • Stage 2 -- Words (3 rounds later): Begin whispering, WIS Save DC 15 to stop
  • Stage 3 -- Commands (1 minute): Speaking loudly, broadcasting. Become a carrier. WIS Save DC 17
  • Stage 4 -- Broadcasting: Shouting. All creatures within 60 ft must save. Character loses next action each turn to broadcasting.
  • Earplugs grant advantage on saves. Silence spell eliminates transmission in its area.
  • The source is a small anomalous object (speaker, music box, etc.) -- destroy it to stop new infections

DM Notes

Run this as a ticking clock. Track rounds carefully. The tension comes from the party splitting between "find the source" and "contain the carriers." If an affected PC reaches Stage 3, the party must choose between gagging their friend or losing more people. The Silence spell is the silver bullet if they have it -- use that as the reward for preparation.

SCP-XXXX-B Keter "The Watcher"
A visual predator. Looking at it delivers recursive thought loops that stack Cognitive Load until catatonia. 18 HP -- easy to kill blind.
Vector Visual Predator
Containment Don't look at it. Engage with eyes closed or averted.
Encounter Combat
MS Toolkit
SCRAMBLE Visual Filter Target Lock
Without MS Fight blind. Close eyes, use sound/touch to locate. AC penalty but survivable.
Threat Summary
The Watcher is physically fragile but cognitively lethal. A single glance starts a recursive loop that consumes processing power -- the character freezes, trapped in an infinite regress of self-referential thought. Each additional glance stacks. At 3 stacks, catatonia. The trap is that it LOOKS harmless, inviting investigation.

Discovery Scenario

The party encounters a still, humanoid figure in a corridor or containment cell. It doesn't move. It doesn't attack. It just watches. The natural response -- looking at it to assess the threat -- is the attack vector.

Mechanics

  • Looking at The Watcher (even briefly): WIS Save DC 14 or gain 1 stack of Cognitive Load
  • Cognitive Load 1: -2 INT, -2 WIS, lose bonus action
  • Cognitive Load 2: -4 INT, -4 WIS, lose bonus action and reaction, speed halved
  • Cognitive Load 3: Catatonic -- incapacitated, prone, unresponsive for 1d4 hours
  • Fighting blind: attacks at disadvantage, target has +2 effective AC
  • The Watcher: AC 10, 18 HP, no attacks, no legendary actions. Moves 15 ft/turn toward nearest sighted creature.
  • Stacks clear at a rate of 1 per short rest

DM Notes

This fight is a puzzle in combat clothing. The answer is simple -- close your eyes and swing -- but players resist it because fighting blind feels wrong. Describe the compulsion to look: "You know it's there. You can hear it breathing. Every instinct says to check where it is." SCRAMBLE is the luxury answer. The budget answer is a blindfold and a mace. Reward creative solutions: fighting by sound, using a mirror at an angle, having one character describe position without looking directly.

SCP-XXXX-C Euclid "The Briefing That Wasn't"
An infohazardous document that plants false mission objectives in the reader's memory. They believe the false briefing is real.
Vector Information / Memory
Containment Destroy the document. Purge false memories from affected personnel.
Encounter Trust / Social
MS Toolkit
SCRAMBLE Scanner Memory Check
Without MS Notice contradictory behavior through RP. Cross-reference orders with command.
Threat Summary
The affected character genuinely believes they received different orders. They will act on those orders with full conviction -- and become defensive when questioned, because from their perspective, they're the only one following the real briefing. The false objectives are designed to compromise the mission: releasing a contained anomaly, eliminating a friendly NPC, or leading the team into a trap.

Discovery Scenario

A document is found at a briefing station, in a dead agent's hand, or slipped into the party's mission materials. It looks identical to a standard Foundation briefing document. One or more characters read it before anyone realizes it's anomalous.

Mechanics

  • Reading the document: WIS Save DC 15 or the false briefing overwrites the real one in memory
  • Affected characters cannot voluntarily disbelieve the false briefing -- it IS their memory
  • SCRAMBLE allows reading without the memetic payload activating
  • Scanner detects memetic contamination in a character: INT Check DC 12
  • Purging false memories: INT Save DC 16 (the affected character must be willing or restrained)
  • Without tools: the only tell is behavioral -- the affected character pursues objectives the others don't recognize

DM Notes

This is a trust encounter. Pull the affected player aside and give them the false briefing privately. They should roleplay their belief sincerely. The other players must figure out that something is wrong through conversation, not dice rolls. The tension peaks when the affected character tries to do something dangerous "because those are the orders." This encounter tests the party's communication and ability to recognize when a teammate has been compromised.

SCP-XXXX-D N/A -- Hazard "Residual Cognitohazard Field"
Invisible ambient cognitohazard contamination left behind after a containment breach. Progressive WIS debuff in affected areas.
Vector Environmental
Containment Ventilation clears contamination. Otherwise, avoid affected areas.
Encounter Navigation
MS Toolkit
Scanner Zone Mapping
Without MS Trial and error. Track where debuffs worsen. Map clean routes manually.
Threat Summary
The field is invisible, odorless, and the debuffs are subtle enough that players may not notice them stacking. Characters moving through contaminated rooms accumulate WIS penalties that lower their saves against every other anomaly on this list. A party that doesn't map the contamination walks into the real encounter already weakened.

Discovery Scenario

The party is traversing a breached containment wing. There are no visible threats. The corridors look clear. But characters begin feeling "off" -- headaches, mild confusion, difficulty concentrating. The contamination is left over from a prior anomalous event.

Mechanics

  • Contaminated rooms: each round of exposure, CON Save DC 12
  • Failure: -1 to WIS (cumulative, max -6)
  • The debuff persists for 1 hour after leaving the contaminated area
  • Scanner maps contamination levels per room: clean / low / medium / high
  • Ventilation systems can be activated from a control room to purge specific wings
  • The contamination is not uniform -- some rooms are clean, creating a pathfinding puzzle
  • Recovery: short rest in a clean area removes 2 points of WIS penalty

DM Notes

This is a force multiplier, not a standalone threat. Place it between the party and something they need to reach. The scanner turns it from frustrating trial-and-error into a tactical mapping exercise. Without the scanner, players must track their own symptoms -- "I felt worse in that hallway" -- and build the map from experience. Pair this with any other encounter on this list for compounding difficulty.

SCP-XXXX-E Keter "The Dreamer"
Attacks during rest. Creates a shared dream with real intel mixed with a planted behavioral trigger. Waking doesn't feel like waking.
Vector Sleep / Dream
Containment Pre-rest mnestic inoculation. Don't sleep in affected areas without it.
Encounter Rest Attack
MS Toolkit
Mnestics Pre-Rest Protocol Dream Anchor
Without MS High-WIS players may catch the second dream layer. Watch for post-rest behavioral changes.
Threat Summary
The Dreamer makes rest dangerous. The shared dream contains useful information -- map fragments, passwords, warnings -- mixed with a behavioral trigger that activates post-waking. The party doesn't know which intel is real and which is the trap. Skipping rest to avoid it means exhaustion penalties stack against them instead.

Discovery Scenario

The party takes a long rest in a breached area. All sleeping characters share the same dream: a Foundation briefing room, a familiar face delivering intel, a map, a code. It feels helpful. It feels real. One detail in the dream is a planted suggestion.

Mechanics

  • All sleeping characters enter the shared dream automatically -- no save to avoid
  • Dream contains 2-3 pieces of real intel and 1 behavioral trigger
  • Detecting the trigger in-dream: WIS Save DC 17 (only characters with WIS 16+ may attempt)
  • Post-waking trigger: activates under specific conditions (seeing a specific anomaly, hearing a phrase, etc.)
  • Triggered character: acts against party interests for 1 round then gets a WIS Save DC 15 to resist
  • Mnestic inoculation before rest: grants immunity to the trigger (dream still occurs, but the trigger is visible and ignorable)
  • Skipping rest: standard exhaustion rules apply

DM Notes

Run the dream as a full scene. Make the intel genuinely useful -- the party should WANT to trust it. The trigger should be subtle: "When you see the red door, open it" or "The researcher in Lab 4 is compromised -- neutralize on sight." The trigger is a time bomb. The party must decide: use the intel (which is real) while knowing one element is a trap, or discard everything and fly blind. The MS pre-rest protocol eliminates the trigger but the party must know to use it BEFORE sleeping -- this rewards preparation.

SCP-XXXX-F Keter "The Mirror"
Copies a person's mind perfectly and broadcasts it from another body. The copy has all memories and believes it is real.
Vector Trust / Cognitive Mimicry
Containment CRV baseline comparison or memory-cutoff interrogation to identify the copy.
Encounter Trust Puzzle
MS Toolkit
Scanner CRV Baseline Identity Verify
Without MS Interrogate for post-contact memories. The copy doesn't have them.
Threat Summary
The copy is perfect. It passes every test the original would pass -- because it IS the original's mind. It doesn't know it's a copy. It will fight, argue, and plead with complete sincerity. The party must determine which version is real with limited information, and the wrong choice means losing a teammate or trusting an anomaly wearing their friend's mind.

Discovery Scenario

A party member who was briefly separated returns -- but so does another version of them from a different direction. Both have the same memories up to the point of separation. Both insist they're real. Both are terrified of the other.

Mechanics

  • The copy is a perfect duplicate at the moment of copying -- same stats, same memories, same personality
  • The copy does NOT have memories formed after the copying event
  • Memory-cutoff interrogation: INT Check DC 14 to design questions that test post-contact knowledge
  • CRV baseline: instant identification -- the scanner reads cognitive signatures, and the copy's is a static snapshot
  • The copy, if identified, may become hostile or desperate: same combat stats as original, +2 to attack rolls from desperation
  • If the copy is not identified and the original is eliminated: the copy continues with the party, functioning normally but potentially compromised at a later date (DM discretion)

DM Notes

This encounter lives or dies on RP. Pull the affected player aside and tell them they don't know which one they are. Both versions should be played with full conviction. The other players must solve it through conversation, not violence. The memory-cutoff technique is the intended non-MS solution: "What happened after you entered the containment wing?" -- the copy will have a gap or fabricate an answer. If the party has a Memetic Specialist, the scanner makes it trivial but removes the social tension. Consider whether you want the easy answer available.

SCP-4781 Euclid "The Residue"
Invisible surface film that transmits dying sensations through bare skin contact. Hand locks to surface. Chain transmission through touch.
Vector Tactile Cognitohazard
Containment Gloves. Don't touch bare surfaces. Decontaminate with standard cleaners.
Encounter Navigation / Rescue
MS Toolkit
Scanner Surface Analysis
Without MS Deduce "gloves" from dead NPC evidence -- bare hands stuck to surfaces, expressions of agony.
Threat Summary
Contact is instant and involuntary -- the hand locks and the victim experiences the recorded death of whoever last touched the surface while dying. The experience is fully immersive: pain, fear, suffocation, whatever killed them. Attempting rescue by grabbing the victim bare-handed chains the transmission. A single ungloved hand can cascade into a total party incapacitation.

Discovery Scenario

The party finds dead Foundation personnel frozen in place, hands pressed flat against walls, door handles, and consoles. Their faces are locked in expressions of extreme distress. One is still alive, whimpering, hand fused to a railing.

Mechanics

  • Bare skin contact with contaminated surface: no save -- hand locks immediately
  • The victim experiences a recorded death: 2d6 psychic damage per round, incapacitated
  • Freeing a victim: STR Check DC 16 (by someone else, using gloves or tools)
  • Chain transmission: touching a locked victim bare-handed locks you to them and starts your own cycle
  • Gloves of any kind provide full protection
  • Scanner detects contaminated surfaces before contact -- maps safe paths
  • Contamination can be cleaned with standard chemical decontaminants (soap, alcohol, bleach)
  • Not all surfaces are contaminated -- the film is patchy and invisible

DM Notes

The clue is in the scene. Dead NPCs with hands stuck to surfaces + no gloves = put on gloves. Reward observant players who examine the bodies before touching anything. The rescue component creates tension: a party member gets stuck, and the others must free them without bare contact while the victim takes psychic damage each round. This is also a navigation puzzle -- with the scanner, you can map safe surfaces. Without it, every door handle is a gamble.

SCP-4782 Keter "The Grief"
Not a creature -- an emotion given will. No body, no form. Injects foreign feelings via d8 table. Your mind stays clear while your body acts on emotions that aren't yours.
Vector Emotional Manipulation
Containment Emotional suppression starves it. Deny it fuel and it weakens.
Encounter Combat / RP
MS Toolkit
Cognitive Strike Emotional Barrier Aura Stun
Without MS Choose to feel nothing -- DC 15 WIS save, costs your action. Starve it out.
Threat Summary
The Grief feeds on failed saves. Each round it injects a random emotion (d8: rage, love, grief, euphoria, paranoia, despair, devotion, terror) and you act on it even though you KNOW it's not yours. Your mind screams "this isn't real" while your body weeps, attacks allies, or freezes in terror. The more it feeds, the stronger the DCs get. A party that fights emotionally feeds it. A party that suppresses starves it -- but suppression costs actions.

Discovery Scenario

The party enters a room where two Foundation guards are locked in a death struggle -- one sobbing while strangling the other, who is laughing hysterically. Neither can explain why. The emotion changes without warning. When the party enters, they feel it too: a sudden, sourceless wave of feeling that doesn't belong to them.

Mechanics

  • Each round, all creatures in range: WIS Save DC 13 (escalates by +1 per failed save across all targets)
  • On failure, roll d8 for emotion:
    1-Rage (attack nearest creature), 2-Love (protect random creature, won't harm), 3-Grief (prone, weeping, lose action),
    4-Euphoria (disadvantage on all checks, can't take threats seriously), 5-Paranoia (attack anyone who approaches),
    6-Despair (no actions, speed 0), 7-Devotion (obey commands of random creature), 8-Terror (dash away, no other actions)
  • Suppression: use your action to attempt WIS Save DC 15 -- on success, immune for 1 round and The Grief takes 1d6 psychic damage
  • Cognitive Strike (MS ability): stuns The Grief's aura for 1d4 rounds, no saves needed during stun
  • The Grief has no physical form: HP 40 (psychic damage only), immune to physical attacks
  • If DC reaches 20, The Grief becomes corporeal for 3 rounds: a vaguely humanoid shimmer, AC 8, vulnerable to all damage

DM Notes

This encounter is about the horror of losing control while remaining aware. Describe the disconnect: "You feel rage flooding your muscles, your fists clench, and you lunge at [ally] -- but behind your eyes, you're screaming that this isn't you." The d8 table makes each round unpredictable. The win condition is counterintuitive: stop fighting it with force and start denying it emotion. The Cognitive Strike is the MS silver bullet. Without it, the party must coordinate suppression -- multiple characters burning actions to starve it while others protect the group from emotion-driven friendly fire. If it reaches DC 20, the brief corporeal window is the desperation play: it becomes killable but the emotional attacks are at their strongest.
